FitLink — Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 14, 2026 · Last updated: June 14, 2026

The short version: FitLink does not collect, store, sell, share, or transmit any of your personal or health data to us or to anyone on our behalf. FitLink has no servers and no account system. Your information stays on your device and moves only between the services you connect (Fitbit and Apple Health). We literally never see it.

1. Who we are

FitLink (“FitLink”, “the app”, “we”, “us”) is an independent iOS application developed by EIGHT SEVEN EIGHT LLC that copies your own fitness data from your Fitbit account into Apple Health. FitLink is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Fitbit LLC, Google LLC, or Apple Inc.

2. What FitLink does

FitLink is a connector. After you sign in to your own Fitbit account, FitLink reads your activity data (steps, active energy/calories, heart rate, and sleep) from Fitbit’s API and writes it into the Apple Health app on your device. That is the entire purpose of the app.

3. Data we collect

None. We do not operate any servers, databases, analytics, advertising, or tracking. We do not have accounts, profiles, or logins on our side. We never receive a copy of your data, and we have no way to identify you.

4. Data that stays on your device

To do its job, FitLink handles the following entirely on your device:

  • Fitbit authorization tokens. When you connect Fitbit, the resulting OAuth access and refresh tokens are stored in the iOS Keychain on your device. They are used only to request your data from Fitbit and are never sent to us.
  • Fitbit activity data. Steps, calories, heart rate, and sleep are fetched from Fitbit directly to your device and written into Apple Health. FitLink does not retain its own copy beyond a local cache used to display your dashboard.
  • App settings. Small preferences (e.g., last-sync time, auto-sync on/off) are stored locally using the system’s standard on-device storage.

FitLink requests write-only access to Apple Health. It writes data into Health and does not read your existing Health data.

5. Third-party services

FitLink connects to services that you control and authenticate to directly:

  • Fitbit (owned by Google LLC) — the source of your activity data. Your use of Fitbit and Google’s handling of your data are governed by their own policies, not ours. See the Fitbit/Google Privacy Policy.
  • Apple Health — the destination for your data. Apple’s handling of Health data is governed by Apple’s policies. See the Apple Privacy Policy.

We are not responsible for, and have no control over, how Fitbit, Google, or Apple collect, use, store, secure, or share your data. Your relationship with each of those companies is separate from your use of FitLink. We encourage you to review their privacy policies.

6. How your data moves

Fitbit’s servers → (over an encrypted HTTPS connection) → your device → Apple Health on your device. No part of this flow passes through any server operated by FitLink, because none exists.

7. Permissions FitLink asks for

  • Apple Health (write): to add your Fitbit data to Health.
  • Notifications (optional): to let you know when a sync finishes or needs attention. Handled locally on your device.
  • Background App Refresh (optional): to sync recent data opportunistically.

You can change or revoke any of these at any time in the iOS Settings app or the Apple Health app.

8. Retention and deletion

Because we store nothing, there is nothing for us to delete. You are always in control:

  • Tap Disconnect Fitbit in FitLink to delete the stored Fitbit tokens from your device.
  • Delete the FitLink app to remove all of its local data.
  • Manage data already written to Apple Health in the Health app (Browse → the relevant category → Data Sources & Access).
  • Revoke FitLink’s access to your Fitbit account at fitbit.com/settings/applications.

9. Children

FitLink is not directed to children and is not intended for use by anyone under the age required to hold a Fitbit account in their jurisdiction. We do not knowingly collect information from children (and, in fact, do not collect information from anyone).

10. Security

Authorization tokens are stored using the iOS Keychain, and all network requests use encrypted HTTPS/TLS. No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

11. Your privacy rights (GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and similar laws)

Laws such as the EU/UK GDPR and the California Consumer Privacy Act give you rights to access, correct, or delete personal data that a business holds about you, and to opt out of its sale or sharing. Because FitLink collects no data, holds no data about you, and never sells or shares any data, there is nothing on our side to access, port, correct, or delete. To exercise rights over data held by the connected services, contact Fitbit/Google or Apple directly.
