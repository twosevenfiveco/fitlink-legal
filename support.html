FitLink — Support

FitLink syncs your Fitbit activity (steps, calories, heart rate, sleep) into Apple Health.

Contact

Email: support@fitlink.app — we aim to reply within a few business days.

Getting started

  1. Open FitLink and tap Connect Fitbit, then sign in to your Fitbit account.
  2. Tap Connect Apple Health and allow write access.
  3. Tap Sync now. Your data appears in the Apple Health app.

Frequently asked questions

Does FitLink collect my data?

No. FitLink has no servers and collects nothing. Data moves from Fitbit directly to your device and into Apple Health. See our Privacy Policy.

My data isn’t showing up in Health.

Make sure you allowed FitLink write access in Health (Health app → Browse → Data Sources & Access → FitLink). Then tap Sync now again. HealthKit only works on a physical iPhone.

Why is my heart rate only a daily value?

Minute-by-minute (“intraday”) heart rate requires special access from Fitbit. Until that is enabled, FitLink syncs daily resting heart rate along with daily steps, calories, and sleep.

Why didn’t my data sync automatically?

Background sync is opportunistic — iOS decides when to run it based on usage, battery, and network. Use Sync now any time for an immediate update.

A large backfill stopped partway.

Fitbit limits how many requests an app can make per hour. Large date ranges can hit that limit and pause — just run the backfill again later to continue.

How do I disconnect or delete my data?

Tap Disconnect Fitbit in FitLink, or delete the app. Manage data already in Health via the Health app. Revoke FitLink’s Fitbit access at fitbit.com/settings/applications.
